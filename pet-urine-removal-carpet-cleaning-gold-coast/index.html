Why generic carpet cleaning doesn't fix pet urine smell

Pet urine is not a surface problem. When a dog or cat urinates on carpet, the liquid soaks through the fibre into the backing and often into the underlay and subfloor. As it dries, bacteria produce ammonia compounds. As it continues to dry, it deposits uric acid crystals.

Uric acid crystals are the core of the problem. They're not water-soluble and don't break down with standard carpet cleaning detergents. When humidity rises — as it does constantly on the Gold Coast — the crystals absorb moisture and re-activate, releasing the odour again. This is why carpets that were "cleaned" still smell after rain or on humid days.

What breaks uric acid crystals down is enzymatic chemistry — biological enzymes that digest the crystal structure rather than trying to dilute or mask it. Standard carpet cleaning skips this step entirely.

The Pet Reset Protocol — how we do it

1

UV inspection

UV light reveals urine deposits invisible to the naked eye — so we treat what's actually there, not just what you can smell or see.

2

Enzymatic pre-spray

Bio-enzymatic solution is applied to all affected areas and allowed to dwell for the time needed to dissolve uric acid crystals. This is the step cheap operators skip.

3

Hot water extraction at 92°C

Commercial-grade truck-mount or portable unit flushes out the digested crystals, bacteria, and residual detergent at high pressure. Carpets dry in 3–5 hours rather than the 24+ hours seen with under-powered rental equipment.

4

pH neutralisation rinse

Urine leaves an alkaline residue that attracts soil and bacteria. A pH-balanced rinse returns the carpet to neutral, preventing rapid re-soiling and the return of odour.

What we can and can't fix

We'll be honest about limitations on-site before charging you:

  • Fresh urine (same-day to a few days) — typically full removal of odour and staining
  • Weeks-old urine on synthetic fibre — strong improvement, usually full removal
  • Multiple-incident areas (repeated same-spot accidents) — usually significant improvement, sometimes complete removal
  • Wool carpets — enzymatic treatment adapted to fibre sensitivity, same result
  • Urine so deeply saturated the underlay needs replacement — we'll tell you on-site
  • Structural subfloor contamination — outside scope of carpet treatment, requires flooring contractor
  • Permanent bleaching/discolouration of coloured carpet — cleaning removes the stain, but dye damage from old urine can't be reversed

Frequently asked questions

Steam cleaning alone doesn't eliminate pet urine odour. You need enzymatic treatment first to break down uric acid crystals, then hot water extraction to flush them out. Without the enzymatic step, heat from steam can permanently bond the proteins in urine to the carpet fibre, making it worse.
With commercial extraction equipment on the Gold Coast, carpets are typically dry in 3–5 hours in well-ventilated rooms. In humid conditions or enclosed rooms, allow 6–8 hours. Running ceiling fans speeds this up significantly.
Old dried urine is harder to remove than fresh, but not impossible. Enzymatic treatment rehydrates the crystals and breaks them down. Multiple-year-old stains in deeply saturated carpet may require sub-floor treatment as well. We assess this on-site before quoting.
Our Pet Reset Protocol is a three-step process: (1) enzymatic pre-spray matched to pet urine chemistry, (2) hot water extraction at 92°C to flush the broken-down crystals, (3) pH neutralisation to prevent residue attracting new soil. Available as a standalone service or add-on to any carpet clean.
