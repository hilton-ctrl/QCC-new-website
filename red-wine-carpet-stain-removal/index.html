Red Wine Stain Removal from Carpet Gold Coast

Red wine stain removal from carpet requires a different approach to general cleaning. Red wine contains tannins and pigments that bond to carpet fibres — hot water alone can set the stain rather than remove it. Specialist pre-treatment followed by hot water extraction gives the best result.

Why Is Red Wine Hard to Remove from Carpet?

Red wine contains tannins — plant-derived compounds that act as natural dyes — along with anthocyanin pigments that bond directly to carpet fibres. When red wine spills on carpet, these compounds begin penetrating the fibre immediately. The longer the spill sits, the deeper the bond.

Applying water or steam to red wine without pre-treatment can push the tannins further into the fibre and spread the stain outward. Common DIY approaches — salt, soda water, white wine — dilute the surface stain but do not remove the tannin bond. Acting quickly and using the right pre-treatment makes a significant difference to the outcome.

Can Old Red Wine Stains Be Removed from Carpet?

Dried red wine is harder to treat than fresh spills. Tannins continue to bond to carpet fibres as they dry and oxidise, making the pigment progressively harder to lift. Old red wine stains can often be significantly lightened, and in some cases fully removed, depending on the carpet fibre and how long the stain has been present.

We assess each stain before starting and give you an honest expectation. We do not make claims we cannot support.

How We Treat Red Wine Carpet Stains

  • Stain assessment — age, fibre type, and history of prior treatment
  • Specialist tannin pre-treatment solution applied to the affected area
  • Dwell time to allow the pre-treatment to break down the tannin bond
  • Hot water extraction to lift the stain and remove treatment residue
  • Re-assessment and re-treatment of any remaining discolouration where possible

Does Red Wine Stain Treatment Work on All Carpet Fibres?

Nylon and polyester synthetic fibres respond better to tannin treatment than natural fibres. Wool is more absorbent and holds tannin pigments more deeply, which can make full removal more difficult and may require multiple treatment passes.

Light-coloured carpets make red wine stains more visible, but the fibre type affects the treatment outcome more than the colour. We will assess the fibre and advise before starting. See our full advanced spot and stain treatment service →

What to Do Before We Arrive

If the spill just happened:

  • Blot gently from the outside edge in toward the centre — do not rub
  • Use a clean white cloth or paper towel to absorb as much liquid as possible
  • Do not apply heat, salt, soda water, or white wine to the stain — these spread the tannin
  • Do not scrub — scrubbing damages carpet fibres and pushes the stain deeper
  • Call us as soon as possible — fresh stains give significantly better results
