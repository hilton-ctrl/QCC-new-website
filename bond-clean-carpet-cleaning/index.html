What Is Bond Carpet Cleaning?

Bond carpet cleaning — also called end of lease carpet cleaning — is professional carpet cleaning carried out by a tenant before vacating a rental property. The aim is to return the carpet to the condition it was in at the start of the tenancy, allowing for fair wear and tear, so the landlord has no grounds to make a carpet cleaning claim against the bond.

In Queensland, the most common cause of bond disputes is carpet condition. Professional hot water extraction cleaning removes the general soiling and odour that accumulates through normal use and gives the best result for inspection.

What Does Bond Carpet Cleaning Include?

  • Hot water extraction cleaning of all carpeted rooms in the property
  • Pre-treatment of high-traffic areas and visible soiling
  • Edge cleaning and attention to corners and door thresholds
  • Deodorisation where needed
  • Optional add-ons: pet stain treatment, advanced spot treatment, or flea treatment where required by the lease
  • Tax invoice provided as evidence for the property manager

Do I Have to Use a Professional for Bond Carpet Cleaning?

In Queensland, landlords cannot legally require tenants to use a professional carpet cleaner if the property did not have professionally cleaned carpets at the start of the tenancy. However, if the carpet is soiled at the end of the lease, the landlord can make a claim against the bond for the cost of professional cleaning.

Most property managers and real estate agents require professional carpet cleaning as a matter of course. If there are pets, stains, or noticeable odour in the carpet, the expected standard is professional cleaning — and in some cases, additional treatment. When Should I Book Bond Carpet Cleaning?

Book as close to your vacate date as possible — the day before or the morning of the inspection is ideal. Carpet cleaned too early risks being soiled again before the agent inspects. We work quickly and can often fit in short-notice bookings for tenants with tight move-out timelines.

Do You Cover Pet Stains and Flea Treatment?

Yes. If you had pets in the property, bond carpet cleaning may need to include pet stain treatment, odour removal, or flea treatment.
