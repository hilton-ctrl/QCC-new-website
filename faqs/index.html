General Carpet Cleaning

A standard 3-bedroom home typically takes 1.5–3 hours depending on the condition of the carpet, the number of rooms, and whether any add-on treatments are required. We will give you a time estimate when you book.

Most carpets are dry within 2–6 hours. Drying time depends on the carpet thickness, fibre type, ventilation, and humidity. Open windows and doors, run a fan or air conditioning, and avoid walking on the carpet with dirty shoes while it dries.

Move smaller items such as chairs, side tables, and ornaments if possible. We work around larger items like sofas and beds. Let us know at booking if there are heavy items you need help moving.

We use hot water extraction, also known as steam cleaning. This method injects hot water and cleaning solution into the carpet pile under pressure and then extracts it — along with dirt, allergens, and residue — using powerful suction. It is the method recommended by most carpet manufacturers for deep cleaning.

Vacuum the carpet before we arrive. Move small furniture items out of the areas to be cleaned. Secure pets in another area. Make sure we have clear access to a water supply and power outlet. Let us know about any stains, odours, or areas of concern when you book.

The cleaning solutions we use are safe for children and pets once the carpet is dry. Keep children and pets off treated areas until fully dry — typically 2–4 hours. If we apply any specialist treatment such as pet enzyme solution, we will advise the specific re-entry time.

Yes. Wool carpets require lower water temperature and pH-appropriate cleaning solutions to avoid shrinkage and fibre damage. We adjust our process for wool and natural fibre carpets and will confirm the method when you book.

For most households, once every 12–18 months is recommended. Homes with pets, children, or high foot traffic may benefit from cleaning every 6–12 months. Regular professional cleaning extends carpet life by removing abrasive particles that degrade fibres over time.

Yes. We provide a tax invoice for every job, which you can use as evidence of professional cleaning for your property manager or records.

We service the Gold Coast and surrounding areas including Robina, Southport, Surfers Paradise, Broadbeach, Nerang, Coomera, Helensvale, Oxenford, Varsity Lakes, Hope Island, Mudgeeraba, Palm Beach, and Currumbin.

Pet Stains & Odour

Standard carpet cleaning will not remove pet odour. Pet urine contains uric acid that bonds to carpet fibres and requires enzyme-based treatment to break down. We offer specialist pet stain and odour treatment as an add-on or standalone service.

Dog urine contains uric acid crystals that bond to carpet fibres and backing. Water-based cleaning reactivates the crystals without removing them — the smell returns as the carpet dries. Enzyme treatment breaks down the uric acid at the molecular level. Read more about dog urine smell removal →

Yes. Cat urine is more concentrated than dog urine and contains felinine, a sulphur compound that produces an intensely pungent smell as it breaks down. Cat urine typically requires a longer enzyme dwell time and may need more than one treatment for heavily affected areas. See our cat urine carpet cleaning page →

Old pet stains are harder to treat than fresh ones because the uric acid bonds more deeply over time. We will assess the area and tell you honestly what result is achievable. In severe cases where the underlay is saturated, full removal may not be possible without underlay replacement.

Yes. Pet odour treatment for upholstery works on the same principle as carpet treatment. We can treat pet-affected couches, lounges, and chairs in the same visit as carpet cleaning. Learn about couch pet odour treatment →

Yes. The enzyme-based solution is safe for pets once the carpet is dry, typically 2–4 hours after treatment. Keep your pets off the treated area until it is fully dry.

Bond & End of Lease Carpet Cleaning

In Queensland, landlords cannot legally require professional carpet cleaning unless the carpet was professionally cleaned at the start of the tenancy. However, if carpets are soiled or stained at the end of the lease, the landlord can claim the cost of professional cleaning from the bond. Most property managers expect professional cleaning as part of a bond clean. See our bond carpet cleaning service →

Bond carpet cleaning includes hot water extraction of all carpeted areas, pre-treatment of high-traffic areas and stains, edge cleaning, and a tax invoice for the property manager. Pet stain treatment, odour removal, or flea treatment can be added where required by the lease.

Book as close to your vacate date as possible — the day before or morning of the inspection is ideal. Carpet cleaned too early risks being soiled again before the agent inspects. We take short-notice bookings when schedule allows.

Most pet-friendly leases in Queensland require a flea treatment certificate from a licensed pest controller when tenants have kept pets. We coordinate combined carpet cleaning and flea treatment as a single visit service and can provide the required documentation. See flea treatment carpet cleaning →

Call us immediately. We will return to re-clean any area the property manager identifies as unsatisfactory at no extra charge, provided the issue is with the cleaning result rather than new soiling after our visit.

Many stains can be reduced or fully removed with specialist pre-treatment. Results depend on stain type, age, and fibre. We assess every stain and give you an honest expectation before we begin — we do not make removal promises we cannot support. See rental carpet stain removal →

Carpet Cleaning & Pest Control

We coordinate combined carpet cleaning and flea treatment as a single visit service. Carpet cleaning alone does not eliminate flea eggs and pupae from the environment. A pest treatment targeting the full flea lifecycle is required for effective control. Learn about flea treatment carpet cleaning →

Hot water extraction removes adult fleas and larvae from carpet fibres. However, flea eggs and pupae can survive in deep pile and under furniture. Combined pest treatment targets the full flea lifecycle — eggs, larvae, pupae, and adults — for effective control.

Queensland's warm climate supports year-round flea populations, and most pet-friendly rental leases require both professional carpet cleaning and pest treatment at end of lease. Many homeowners also book them together as a one-stop refresh after flea treatment to remove dead insects and allergens from carpets. See our carpet cleaning and pest control service →

Yes. You and your pets should vacate for the duration of treatment and for the re-entry time specified by the pest technician — typically 2–4 hours. When we book a combined service, we coordinate carpet cleaning and pest treatment so you only need to vacate once.

No. Flea pupae in cocoons are resistant to treatment and continue to hatch for 2–4 weeks after application. As they emerge, they contact the treated surface and die. Continue vacuuming regularly during this period to stimulate hatch and accelerate the process. This is normal and expected.

Couch & Upholstery Cleaning

Yes. Couch and upholstery cleaning can be added to any carpet cleaning booking. We clean fabric sofas, lounges, sectionals, and chairs using methods appropriate to the fabric type. See our couch and upholstery cleaning service →

Yes. Enzyme-based upholstery treatment breaks down the uric acid and organic compounds causing pet odour at the fibre level. Deodoriser sprays and surface cleaning mask the smell temporarily — they do not remove the source. See couch pet odour treatment →

Fabric upholstery typically dries within 2–4 hours depending on the fabric thickness, cushion density, and ventilation. Opening windows and running a fan speeds up drying significantly.

Our primary service is fabric upholstery cleaning. Leather couches require different conditioning products and methods — contact us to discuss your specific piece before booking.

Check the fabric care code tag on your couch. Code W (water-based cleaning) and WS (water or solvent) are safe for wet cleaning. Code S (solvent only) or X (vacuum only) require dry methods. We assess the care code before applying any treatment and will advise if your fabric needs a different approach.

Stain Removal

Many stains can be significantly reduced or fully removed with specialist pre-treatment. Results depend on the stain type, age, carpet fibre, and whether previous DIY treatment has set the stain. We assess each stain and give you an honest expectation before we begin. See our advanced stain treatment service →

In most cases yes, particularly for recent stains. Red wine contains tannins that bond to carpet fibres — specialist pre-treatment is needed before hot water extraction. Do not apply salt or soda water as these can spread the stain. See red wine stain removal →

Yes, particularly for fresh stains. Blood is a protein stain that requires cold pre-treatment — hot water sets the protein permanently into the fibre. Never apply hot water or steam to a blood stain before pre-treatment. See blood stain removal →

Some stains cause permanent dye damage to carpet fibres that cannot be reversed by cleaning. These include bleach, some toilet cleaners, hair dye, and certain industrial chemicals. Very old stains that have been set by repeated DIY cleaning may also not fully lift. We will tell you honestly at assessment if a stain cannot be removed.

Blot gently with a clean cloth from the outside of the stain inward — do not rub. Avoid applying consumer stain removers, as some set the stain or interfere with professional pre-treatment. Call us as soon as possible — fresh stains respond much better than dried ones.
