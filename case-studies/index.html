Case 01 Pet Stain & Bond Clean

Dog Urine in Rental Carpet — Three-Year Tenancy, Two Dogs

Coomera · 4-bedroom house · End of lease inspection due in 48 hours

Result after hot water extraction and pet urine enzyme treatment — master bedroom and hallway.

⚠️ The Problem

The tenant had lived in a 4-bedroom rental in Coomera for three years with two large dogs. The property manager had flagged the carpet in the master bedroom and hallway during a routine inspection six months earlier. At vacate, the carpet had multiple areas of visible urine staining and a persistent odour that the tenant had unsuccessfully tried to treat with supermarket enzyme spray.

The inspection was booked for 48 hours after vacate. The tenant called us the day they handed back keys.

🔍 What Was Done

  • UV inspection of all carpeted areas to map the full contamination — found additional spots in the second bedroom not reported by the tenant
  • Commercial-grade enzyme solution applied to all affected areas with extended dwell time — 25 minutes given the age and depth of the staining
  • Hot water extraction of treated areas at standard temperature
  • Re-application and extraction on three heavily saturated spots in the hallway
  • Full house extraction clean of remaining rooms as part of the bond clean
  • Assessment after drying — one small area in the hallway showed residual light staining from an old treatment that had partially set the stain. Tenant was advised before we left.

✓ The Result

"Odour was eliminated across all treated areas. Visible staining was removed from the master bedroom and second bedroom. The one hallway spot lightened significantly but retained faint discolouration from the previous DIY treatment — we documented this with photos for the tenant. The property manager passed the carpet at inspection."

Case 02 Bond Clean + Pest Control

End of Lease Carpet Cleaning and Flea Treatment — Cat Household

Helensvale · 3-bedroom unit · Lease required pest treatment certificate

Stairway carpet after hot water extraction — part of full-property bond clean.

⚠️ The Problem

A tenant in a 3-bedroom unit in Helensvale was moving out after two years with two cats. Their lease contained a standard Queensland pet clause requiring professional carpet cleaning and a licensed pest treatment with certificate before vacating. The tenant had not realised the pest treatment needed to be by a licensed operator — a DIY flea bomb would not satisfy the property manager.

They also had a two-storey unit with carpeted stairs, which had received heavy daily foot traffic and showed significant soiling on the upper step edges.

🔍 What Was Done

  • Coordinated licensed pest treatment and carpet cleaning as a combined visit — tenant vacated for the day
  • Pest treatment of all floor areas including hard floors, skirting boards, and under furniture, targeting the full flea lifecycle
  • Full hot water extraction carpet clean of all rooms, hallway, and stairs
  • Pre-treatment of stair edges and high-traffic landing area
  • UV inspection for cat urine — one area in the main bedroom confirmed and treated with enzyme solution before extraction
  • Pest treatment certificate issued; tax invoice provided for both services

✓ The Result

"Both services completed in a single visit. Pest certificate and carpet cleaning invoice provided to the property manager same day. Tenant reported the inspection passed without issue and the bond was returned in full. The stair carpet came up significantly better than expected — the pre-treatment on the step edges made the difference."

Case 03 Stain Removal

Red Wine Stain on Light Wool Carpet — Three Weeks Old

Broadbeach · Owner-occupied apartment · Wool loop pile carpet

Lounge carpet after tannin pre-treatment and hot water extraction — light-coloured wool pile.

⚠️ The Problem

A red wine spill on a light cream wool loop pile carpet in a Broadbeach apartment had been sitting for approximately three weeks. The owner had applied soda water and then a commercial stain remover within the first few hours, which had spread the stain from a small pour to a roughly 30cm smear. The commercial product had partially dried in the fibre, and the tannin pigment had had time to bond into the wool.

Wool loop pile is one of the more challenging carpet types for red wine removal — the natural protein structure of the fibre holds tannin pigment more tenaciously than synthetic fibre, and the loop construction traps residue deeper in the pile.

🔍 What Was Done

  • Fibre identification confirmed — wool loop pile. Adjusted approach to use lower water temperature and pH-appropriate pre-treatment to avoid fibre shrinkage or colour damage
  • Specialist tannin pre-treatment applied to the stain and the surrounding area that had been spread by the soda water application
  • Extended dwell time — 20 minutes given the stain age and fibre type
  • Low-temperature extraction to lift the pre-treatment and broken-down tannin residue
  • Re-assessment — central area clear; faint shadow at the outer edge of the spread zone. Second application to the shadow area, further dwell, re-extraction
  • Client advised before we left that a very faint residual shadow may become visible as the carpet dried fully — this is the limit of what is achievable on a three-week tannin stain in wool

✓ The Result

"The main stain area came up clean. The outer shadow from the soda water spread was reduced by around 80% — a faint trace remained after drying but was not visible at standing height. The client was satisfied. We would have achieved a better result on a fresher stain before the soda water was applied."

Case 04 Couch & Upholstery

Sectional Sofa Pet Odour — Dog Household, Four Years of Use

Robina · Family home · Large L-shaped fabric sectional

Fabric sectional during extraction cleaning after enzyme odour treatment — Robina family home.

⚠️ The Problem

A large L-shaped fabric sectional in a Robina family home had been used daily by two medium-sized dogs for approximately four years. The fabric had a persistent wet-dog smell that was noticeable from the room entrance. The owners had tried a commercial upholstery deodoriser spray multiple times — the smell returned within a few days each time.

The sofa had a W (water-based cleaning) fabric code. The cushion covers were not removable. The foam cushions beneath the fabric were also contributing to odour retention.

🔍 What Was Done

  • Fabric code confirmed — W rated, safe for wet extraction
  • Enzyme-based pet odour treatment applied to all fabric surfaces including seat cushions, back cushions, armrests, and the base sections where dogs rested
  • Dwell time of 15 minutes to allow the enzyme solution to penetrate the fabric and reach the foam beneath
  • Full upholstery extraction — seat cushions extracted both on the surface and by pressing the wand firmly to draw solution through the fabric into the foam layer
  • Repeat treatment on the two seat sections with the heaviest use — these showed the most odour on re-assessment
  • Drying time estimated at 3–4 hours with good ventilation; owners were advised to leave windows open and avoid placing dogs on the sofa until fully dry

✓ The Result

"Odour was eliminated. The client called two days later to confirm the smell had not returned — it had not. The fabric also came up visibly cleaner, with general soiling and pet hair residue removed by the extraction. Combined with the carpet clean done in the same visit, the whole house smelled noticeably different."

Case 05 Carpet Cleaning

Heavily Soiled Investment Property — Returned After Long Tenancy

Nerang · 4-bedroom house · Landlord preparing for re-let

Hot water extraction in progress — high-traffic living areas required multiple passes.

⚠️ The Problem

A landlord in Nerang had a 4-bedroom house returned after a five-year tenancy. The outgoing tenants had cleaned to a basic standard but the carpet had accumulated five years of heavy-family-use soiling. The living room and main hallway were the worst affected — a dark grey path had formed in the carpet pile along all traffic routes from the general compaction and embedded dirt. The carpet itself was in reasonable structural condition but looked aged beyond its years.

The landlord needed the property re-let quickly and wanted to delay carpet replacement if the cleaning result was acceptable. We were asked to give an honest assessment before starting.

🔍 What Was Done

  • Pre-inspection and honest assessment provided — carpet was 7 years old and heavily trafficked, but the fibre was intact and replacement was not essential if cleaning improved the result sufficiently
  • Heavy-duty pre-treatment applied to all high-traffic areas and the main living room paths before any extraction
  • Double-pass hot water extraction on the living room and hallway — first pass to lift the surface soiling, second pass to clear the pre-treatment and deeper residue
  • Standard single-pass extraction on bedrooms and remaining areas
  • Grooming of pile direction after extraction to improve appearance
  • Re-assessment after drying — landlord was invited to inspect before we left

✓ The Result

"The traffic paths in the living room lifted significantly — the carpet looked like a different age on the day. After drying, some residual shadowing was visible in the most compacted sections of the hallway, which we disclosed honestly. The landlord decided cleaning was sufficient and the property was re-let without carpet replacement. The result saved several thousand dollars versus a full recarpet."
