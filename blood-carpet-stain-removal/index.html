Blood Stain Removal from Carpet Gold Coast

Blood stain removal from carpet requires cold water treatment — warm or hot water permanently sets protein-based stains by bonding them to carpet fibres. Specialist cold pre-treatment dissolves the protein structure so it can be extracted cleanly.

Why Is Blood Different from Other Carpet Stains?

Blood is a protein-based stain. When heat is applied to blood — even warm water — the protein denatures and bonds permanently to the carpet fibre. This makes blood one of the few stains where the most common cleaning instinct (hot water, steam) actively makes the situation worse.

Standard carpet cleaning equipment operates at high water temperatures. A carpet cleaner that applies hot water to a blood stain without cold pre-treatment will set the stain before extraction, often permanently. Cold enzyme pre-treatment that digests the protein structure must come first.

Can Old Blood Stains Be Removed from Carpet?

Fresh blood responds significantly better than dried blood. As blood dries, the protein bonds progressively more deeply to the fibre, making it harder to fully dissolve and extract. Old blood stains can often be significantly reduced and in some cases fully removed, depending on the fibre type and whether heat was previously applied to the stain.

If hot water or steam was applied to the stain before our visit, the stain may be partially set and more difficult to treat. We assess and advise honestly before starting.

How We Remove Blood Stains from Carpet

  • Cold enzyme or protein digester pre-treatment applied to the stain
  • Dwell time to allow the enzyme to break down the protein structure
  • Cold water rinse to lift the dissolved protein from the fibre
  • Hot water extraction to complete removal and dry the area
  • Re-assessment and re-treatment of any remaining discolouration

What Should I Do Before You Arrive?

  • Use cold water only — never warm or hot water on a blood stain
  • Blot gently from the outside edge inward with a clean cold damp cloth — do not rub
  • Do not apply heat in any form — no steam, no hair dryer, no warm water
  • Do not use consumer stain products that require warm or hot water activation
  • Call us as soon as possible — fresh blood is significantly easier to remove than dried blood
