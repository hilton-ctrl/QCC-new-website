Which Carpet Stains Are Common in Rental Properties?

After a tenancy, carpet staining tends to fall into a few categories that property managers commonly identify at outgoing inspection:

  • Pet urine stains — dog, cat, and other pets
  • Red wine and other beverage spills
  • Coffee and tea staining
  • Food staining — grease, sauces, curry
  • Vomit staining
  • Blood staining
  • Mud and dirt tracked into high-traffic areas
  • Crayon, ink, and marker stains
  • General high-traffic soiling and discolouration

Can All Rental Carpet Stains Be Removed Before Inspection?

Not always. Results depend on the stain type, how long it has been there, the carpet fibre, and whether DIY cleaning has been attempted previously. Fresh stains respond significantly better than old, set-in stains. Some stains — particularly those involving direct dye damage — cause permanent discolouration that cannot be reversed by any cleaning method.

What Does Rental Carpet Stain Removal Involve?

  • Pre-inspection and visual assessment of all stained areas
  • Identification of stain type to select the appropriate pre-treatment
  • Specialist pre-treatment solution applied with dwell time
  • Hot water extraction to lift the stain and remove the treatment residue
  • Re-assessment and re-treatment of persistent areas where possible

Does Stain Removal Replace Bond Carpet Cleaning?

No. Stain removal is a targeted treatment for specific problem areas. Bond carpet cleaning — hot water extraction of the full carpet area — is still required as the baseline for returning the carpet to an acceptable condition. Stain pre-treatment is carried out as part of the same visit, so you do not need to book separately.

What If Stains Cannot Be Fully Removed?

Queensland tenancy law recognises fair wear and tear, and stains that occurred during normal use of the property — particularly where a genuine professional attempt has been made — are relevant to any bond dispute. We provide a tax invoice as documented evidence of professional treatment.

If you are concerned about specific stains ahead of your inspection, call us before you move out so we can assess the situation and advise what is realistic.
