What fabric types do you clean?

We clean all standard residential upholstery fabrics including microfibre, polyester, cotton blends, nylon, and most synthetic fabrics. Before treating, we check the manufacturer's cleaning code on the furniture to confirm the appropriate method.

  • Microfibre - one of the most common fabric types on the Gold Coast, responds very well to hot water extraction
  • Polyester and polyester blends - durable and generally easy to clean
  • Cotton and cotton blends - cleaned with care to avoid shrinkage
  • Nylon - hard-wearing and receptive to professional cleaning

We do not clean leather, suede, or velvet upholstery - these require specialist dry-cleaning methods. Furniture marked with a dry-clean-only code is also outside our scope.

Can you remove pet odour from a couch?

Yes. Pet odour in upholstery - particularly dog and cat urine - is one of the most common reasons Gold Coast homeowners contact us for couch cleaning. Standard cleaning alone often doesn't fully resolve deep pet odour because the residue has soaked through the fabric and into the foam beneath.

We offer a specialist pet stain and odour treatment for upholstery that uses an enzyme-based treatment to break down odour-causing residues at a deeper level than surface cleaning alone. This is applied after the initial clean and significantly improves outcomes for heavily pet-affected furniture.

How does couch and upholstery cleaning work?

We use a portable hot water extraction system for upholstery - the same extraction principle used for carpets, adapted for fabric surfaces. The process:

  • Pre-inspection - fabric type identified, cleaning code checked
  • Pre-treatment spray applied to loosen dirt, oils, and surface soiling
  • Hot water extraction - injected and immediately extracted, lifting soiling out of the fabric
  • Spot treatment for any visible stains
  • Post-clean assessment - re-treated where required

How long does it take and how long to dry?

A standard 3-seater sofa takes around 30-45 minutes to clean. A large L-shaped sectional takes 60-90 minutes. Drying time is typically 2-4 hours depending on ventilation and humidity - Gold Coast's warm climate generally helps furniture dry quickly.

Open windows and use fans to speed up drying. Avoid sitting on the furniture until it's fully dry to prevent re-soiling.

Do you clean upholstery in rental properties?
