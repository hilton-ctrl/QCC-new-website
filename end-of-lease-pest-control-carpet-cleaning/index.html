Why Is Pest Control Required at End of Lease?

Most pet-friendly residential leases in Queensland include a condition requiring the tenant to arrange a licensed pest treatment — typically a flea treatment — before vacating. This requirement exists because fleas can establish a lifecycle in carpet fibres and flooring that persists long after the pet leaves the property.

Flea eggs and pupae are resistant to physical removal and can remain dormant in carpet for weeks. If untreated, they hatch when new occupants move in and vibrations trigger emergence. Property managers routinely require a pest treatment certificate from a licensed controller as documented evidence that treatment was carried out. Without it, the cost of pest control is typically deducted from the bond.

  • Flea treatment of carpet and hard floor areas carried out by a licensed pest controller
  • Treatment of skirting boards and other flea harborage areas
  • Flea treatment certificate issued to the tenant for the property manager
  • Tax invoice for carpet cleaning as evidence of professional service

We coordinate carpet cleaning and pest treatment as a combined visit so you vacate the property once and get both completed in the same session.

Do I Need Both Carpet Cleaning and Pest Control?

Most leases require both, and for good reason — they address different problems. Carpet cleaning removes soiling, odour, pet hair, and general residue left by the tenancy. Pest control addresses the flea lifecycle in the carpet and environment.

Carpet cleaning alone does not eliminate flea eggs or pupae, which are embedded in the pile and not removed by extraction. Pest control alone leaves the carpet soiled from the tenancy. Property managers expect both, and both contribute to passing the outgoing inspection. Learn more about flea treatment and carpet cleaning →

What If I Have Not Had Pets — Is Pest Control Still Required?

It depends on your lease. Some tenancy agreements require pest control at the end of every lease regardless of pet occupation, particularly in areas where pest pressure is higher. Others only require it when pets were kept.

Check your lease agreement carefully. If you are unsure, call us — we can help you read what your lease actually requires before you book.

How Long Before Move-Out Should I Book?

Book as close to your vacate date as possible — ideally 1–2 days before the outgoing inspection. Vacate the property first so carpet cleaning and pest control can be done without your belongings present. Both services are completed in a single visit, and you should allow 2–4 hours after pest treatment before re-entering the property.
