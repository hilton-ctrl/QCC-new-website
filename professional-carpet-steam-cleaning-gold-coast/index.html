What "steam cleaning" actually means

Most people say "steam cleaning" but what they're describing is hot water extraction — the industry standard method for deep carpet cleaning. True steam is vapour only and doesn't extract. Hot water extraction works differently: heated water is injected into the carpet pile under pressure, agitating and dissolving soil, then immediately extracted by powerful suction along with the dirty water, detergent, and loosened debris.

The temperature matters. At 92°C, the extraction water kills dust mites and bacteria that survive cooler washes. Rental machines typically reach 40–60°C — enough for surface cleaning, not enough for the same hygiene result.

Frequently asked questions

"Steam cleaning" is the common name most people use, but professional carpet cleaning is technically hot water extraction. True steam (vapour only) doesn't extract — it just applies heat. Hot water extraction injects heated water at pressure into the carpet pile and immediately vacuums it back out, taking soil and detergent with it. The terms are used interchangeably in the industry.
With commercial-grade extraction equipment, carpets are typically dry in 3–6 hours on the Gold Coast. Humidity affects this — on humid days, run ceiling fans and open windows. Carpets cleaned with rental or portable domestic machines can take 24+ hours due to lower extraction power.
For a household with no pets, every 12–18 months is a reasonable maintenance interval. Homes with pets or children, or high-traffic areas, benefit from every 6–12 months. Rental properties should be cleaned before and after each tenancy.
Rental machines use lower water temperature, lower extraction pressure, and deposit more water into the carpet than they remove. Professional equipment extracts more effectively, leaving carpets drier and cleaner. The drying time difference alone (3–6 hours vs 24+) is significant in Gold Coast humidity where longer wet time increases mould risk.
